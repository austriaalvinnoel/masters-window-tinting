What are the window tint laws in New York?

+
NY State law requires front side windows to allow at least 70% light in. Rear side and rear windows have no legal minimum for private vehicles â€” you can go as dark as 5% on them. We'll always advise you on exactly what's legal and inspect your install against current NYS code before you leave.

How long does a window tint installation take?

+
A standard full-car tint takes about 2â€“3 hours for a sedan, 3â€“4 hours for SUVs and trucks. Windshield-only or partial installs can be done in under 2 hours. We'll give you an accurate time estimate when you schedule.

When can I roll my windows down after tinting?

+
We recommend waiting at least 3â€“5 days before rolling your windows down, and up to 7 days in cooler or humid weather. This allows the adhesive to fully cure to the glass. Rolling them down too early can cause peeling at the edges.

What is computer-cut tinting and why does it matter?

+
Computer-cut means we use a precision plotter with a database of exact window patterns for your specific vehicle year, make, and model. The film arrives pre-cut to exact dimensions. This means no knives ever touch your vehicle, no risk of scratching trim, and cleaner edges than hand-cut installations. It's the industry standard for quality shops.

Is ceramic tint really worth the extra cost?

+
For most Long Island drivers â€” yes, absolutely. Summers here are brutal. Ceramic film rejects up to 98% of infrared heat, meaning your car stays dramatically cooler when parked and your A/C isn't working as hard. It also won't fade, bubble, or turn purple over time the way dyed films can. The difference is noticeable from the first hot day.

Will tint interfere with my GPS, E-ZPass, or phone signal?

+
Standard ceramic films have zero metallic content, so they won't interfere with any signals â€” GPS, E-ZPass, Apple CarPlay, or cellular. Older metallic films can cause issues, which is one reason ceramic is worth the upgrade. We'll always recommend the right film for your vehicle and usage.

Do you offer tint removal?

+
Yes â€” we remove old, bubbling, or discolored tint before any new installation. If you have existing film from another shop that needs to come off first, we handle that as part of the prep process. Call us and we'll let you know the cost based on the condition of your current film.

What does the lifetime warranty cover?

+
Lifetime warranty covers bubbling, peeling, and fading with full replacement of failed material and workmanship. No refunds.
